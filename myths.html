The myth of Icarus is a famous Greek legend about ambition and hubris. Icarus and his father Daedalus were imprisoned on the island of Crete, and to escape, Daedalus crafted wings made of feathers and wax for both of them. Daedalus warned Icarus not to fly too close to the sun, but Icarus, caught up in the thrill of flight, ignored the warning. The sun melted the wax on his wings, causing Icarus to fall into the sea and drown. The lesson is greed!
